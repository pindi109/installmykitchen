## 1. Executive Summary

installmykitchen.co.uk has exceptionally strong technical foundations and a well-structured programmatic architecture. The site generates clean URLs, crawls efficiently, loads quickly, and targets a logical set of keywords across locations and retailer combinations. These are genuine competitive advantages that most small local businesses do not have.

However, the site currently sits in an uncomfortable middle ground: it has the structural characteristics of a content network but the authority signals of a brand-new business. Google — and increasingly the large language models that surface local recommendations — cannot distinguish between a highly capable independent installer with years of real experience and a thin affiliate site. Without the authority layer, the site will plateau.

The gap is not technical. It is human. The site does not yet show who Pindi Sahota is, what he has built, who has trusted him, or why a homeowner in Kenilworth should choose him over a national retailer's fitting team. Every improvement in this audit addresses that gap.

The work is split across five passes. Pass 1 — underway — targets the highest-leverage changes: enriching location pages with genuinely local content, building the missing high-intent money pages, creating LLM-optimised Q&A pages, and establishing homepage authority. Passes 2 through 5 build the authority layer progressively through case studies, review integration, deep internal linking, schema markup, and CRO improvements.

A realistic target after all five passes: move from 6.8/10 overall to 8.5+/10, with meaningful organic rankings for money keywords across the West Midlands and Warwickshire.

## 2. Critical Issues — Priority 1: Do First

These are the issues that actively suppress rankings right now. None require new content research. All require existing content to be made real.

### 2.1 Location Pages Are Formula-Driven

Every location page currently swaps the place name into the same template. The content about kitchen installation in Nuneaton is identical in substance to the content about kitchen installation in Leamington Spa. Google's quality raters and its algorithms can detect this. The pages rank poorly for localised terms because they carry no genuine local signal.

**Fix:** Each page must contain information that is only true of that specific location. See Section 4 for the full per-area data brief.

### 2.2 The Homepage Establishes No Authority

The homepage currently describes the service category rather than the business. A visitor learns what kitchen installation is, but not who Pindi Sahota is, how many kitchens he has fitted, what they look like, or what past customers say. This is the single most damaging issue on the site for conversion and for Google's EEAT evaluation.

**Fix:** See Section 3 for the full homepage rebuild brief.

### 2.3 EEAT Is Weak Throughout

Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness (EEAT) is how Google evaluates pages that affect significant decisions — home improvements sit firmly in this category. The site currently signals none of these. Pindi Sahota has no visible biography, no credentials displayed, no portfolio, and no reviews surfaced on the site.

**Fix:** See Section 7 for the full EEAT build plan.

### 2.4 High-Intent Money Pages Are Missing

The pages that convert — cost pages, comparison pages, checklist pages — are absent. These are the pages people search for when they have money in their hand and are ready to book. Their absence means the site captures research traffic but not buying traffic.

**Fix:** See Section 5 for the full missing pages brief.

### 2.5 Internal Linking Is Too Thin

Pages currently link to one another only through navigation. There are no contextual in-content links. A location page for Leamington Spa does not link to the Leamington Spa + Howdens page, does not reference the cost guide, does not mention the survey page. Google uses internal linking to understand topical depth and page relationships. Thin linking signals a thin site.

**Fix:** See Section 8 for the full internal linking strategy.

## 3. Homepage Fixes Required

The homepage is the highest-authority page on the domain. Every improvement here compounds across the whole site. It needs to change from a service description to a proof-of-business.

### 3.1 Real Projects Section

Add a dedicated section above or near the fold: "Kitchens I've Installed". Display a minimum of 20 completed kitchen projects. For each project, include:

- One strong photo (before and/or after)
- Location (e.g., "Kenilworth", "Rugby")
- Retailer (e.g., "Wren", "Howdens", "IKEA")
- Brief descriptor (e.g., "Shaker-style in a 1930s semi", "Handleless gloss in a new build")

This section serves two purposes: it proves real work has been done, and it generates longtail visual queries and image search traffic.

### 3.2 Review Integration

Surface genuine customer reviews directly on the homepage. Do not rely on a link to Google Maps. Integrate:

- **Google Reviews**: embed or display via API, minimum 5 visible on page load
- **Trustpilot or Facebook Reviews**: secondary source for redundancy and credibility
- Show the star rating prominently (feeds rich snippet eligibility)
- Include reviewer name, location, and date — these matter to Google quality raters

If review volume is currently low, this should trigger an immediate review acquisition campaign (see Section 7).

### 3.3 Installer Credentials

Add a credentials strip to the homepage — a horizontal band of trust markers:

- Trade membership badges (BIKBBI if held, or equivalent)
- Public liability insurance (state the level — e.g., £2m)
- Workmanship guarantee (state the years)
- Fully qualified / time-served (state the years of experience)

These should appear as visual elements with brief text, not buried in a paragraph.

### 3.4 Case Studies

Create minimum 4 full case studies and link to them from the homepage. Each case study must include:

- Full project narrative (500+ words)
- Customer location and brief about the property
- Retailer and kitchen range used
- Timeline from survey to sign-off
- Approximate cost range (even a band — "£800–£1,200 fitting only" is useful)
- Any problems encountered and how they were resolved
- Photos throughout
- Customer quote or testimonial

Case studies are the single most powerful EEAT signal for a local tradesperson's website. See Section 7 for the full brief.

### 3.5 Authority Narrative

Add a dedicated "Why trust us" section — not a generic paragraph about kitchen installation, but a specific statement about Pindi Sahota:

- Years in the trade
- Approximate number of kitchens installed (even a rounded figure)
- Types of kitchens specialised in
- Geographic coverage and why (Coventry base, local knowledge)
- What makes the approach different from retailer fitting teams

This section is what a journalist, a Google quality rater, or an LLM looks at to determine whether to treat the site as a genuine expert source.

## 4. Location Page Transformation

## 5. Missing Money Pages to Create

These pages target the specific search queries people use when they are close to booking. They are the highest-converting page types for a local trade service. None currently exist on the site.

### /removal/ — Kitchen Removal

**Target keywords:** "kitchen removal cost", "how much to remove old kitchen", "kitchen removal Coventry", "cost to strip out old kitchen"

**Page brief:** Comprehensive guide to kitchen removal costs in the West Midlands. Cover what removal includes (units, worktops, appliances, flooring if applicable), what affects the price (size, number of units, distance to skip/disposal), typical day rate for removal only, whether removal is included in installation quotes or charged separately, what happens to old units (skip, donation, tip), asbestos risks in older properties, and how to prepare for a removal visit.

Include a clear cost table: typical removal-only costs for different kitchen sizes. Optimise for local search with Coventry and Warwickshire pricing context.

### /refit/ — Kitchen Refit / Renovation Cost

**Target keywords:** "kitchen refit cost", "kitchen renovation cost", "how much does a kitchen refit cost UK", "kitchen renovation Coventry"

**Page brief:** Distinguish between a refit (keeping existing layout, replacing units and worktops) and a full renovation (moving plumbing, changing layout). Provide cost breakdowns for each scenario. Cover fitting costs only (supply and fit vs fitting only). Include factors that affect refit costs: number of units, worktop material, appliance installation complexity, existing pipework condition. Coventry and Warwickshire pricing context throughout.

### /checklist/ — Kitchen Installation Checklist

**Target keywords:** "kitchen installation checklist", "preparing for kitchen installation", "what to do before kitchen fitter arrives", "kitchen fitting preparation"

**Page brief:** A downloadable or printable checklist for homeowners. Cover what to do in the weeks before, the days before, and on the day of installation. Include: clearing the room, isolating water/gas/electric, removing wall tiles if required, confirming appliance delivery, checking the delivery is correct and complete, organising access, temporary kitchen arrangements, protecting flooring in surrounding areas, and what to expect on Day 1. This page should be genuinely useful and sharable — it will attract links from kitchen forums and retailer advice pages.

### /mistakes/ — Common Kitchen Installation Mistakes

**Target keywords:** "kitchen installation mistakes", "common kitchen fitting problems", "kitchen fitter mistakes to avoid", "kitchen fitting gone wrong"

**Page brief:** A frank guide to the most common problems seen in kitchen installations — both from DIY attempts and poor-quality fitting teams. Cover: incorrect measurements before ordering, wrong unit heights for appliances, poor plumbing connections, inadequate ventilation, insufficient electrical circuits, worktop joins not properly sealed, doors hung at the wrong height, end panels omitted. For each mistake: what it is, why it happens, what it costs to fix, how to avoid it. This page positions Pindi Sahota as the expert who catches these problems before they happen.

### /day-rate/ — Kitchen Fitter Day Rate

**Target keywords:** "kitchen fitter day rate", "how much does a kitchen fitter charge per day", "kitchen fitter hourly rate UK", "kitchen installation labour cost per day"

**Page brief:** Honest breakdown of what kitchen fitters charge per day in the UK, with specific reference to the West Midlands. Cover: typical daily rate range (solo fitter vs team), what a day rate includes and excludes, why some fitters quote per day and others per project, how to compare quotes fairly, red flags in very cheap quotes, what a realistic total fitting cost looks like for a typical kitchen. This is a transparency page — it builds trust by giving people the numbers rather than forcing them to book a quote just to find out.

### /near-me/ — Kitchen Fitter Near Me

**Target keywords:** "kitchen fitter near me", "kitchen installer near me", "kitchen fitter near me Coventry", "local kitchen fitter Coventry Warwickshire"

**Page brief:** A hub page optimised for "near me" intent. Explain the service area clearly (all 25 locations), explain why local knowledge matters (housing stock, access, local suppliers), explain what sets an independent local fitter apart from a national retailer's team, and link out to each location page. Include a map embed showing the coverage area. This page should rank for any "[town] + kitchen fitter near me" query within the region.

### /timeline/ — Kitchen Installation Timeline

**Target keywords:** "kitchen installation timeline", "how long does kitchen fitting take", "how long does kitchen installation take", "kitchen fitting duration"

**Page brief:** A realistic guide to how long a kitchen installation takes from first contact to finished kitchen. Cover: the survey visit, ordering lead times, delivery day, Day 1 of installation, typical duration for different kitchen sizes (small galley up to large open-plan), what causes delays, how to plan around it, what the homeowner needs to arrange (temporary kitchen, eating arrangements, access for deliveries). **Target keywords:** "kitchen installation cost calculator", "kitchen fitting cost calculator", "how much will my kitchen cost to install"

## 6. LLM SEO Pages Needed

Large language models (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude) are increasingly used by homeowners to research tradespeople. When someone asks "who installs Wren kitchens independently?" — an LLM synthesises an answer from web content. Pages written in a clear Q&A format with direct, citable answers are the most likely to be used as source material.

Each page below should be written in natural, conversational language. Open with the question as a heading. Answer it directly in the first two sentences. Then expand with detail, local context, and a call to action.

### Target LLM Questions

**"Who installs Howdens kitchens?"**
Path: /guides/who-installs-howdens-kitchens/
Answer: Howdens is trade-only — they sell to builders and fitters, not direct to the public. An independent kitchen fitter is the standard route. Explain how to find a Howdens-accredited fitter, what to look for, and position installmykitchen.co.uk as the local answer in Coventry and Warwickshire.

**"Can I get someone else to install my Wren kitchen?"**
Path: /guides/independent-wren-kitchen-installer/
Answer: Yes. You are not obliged to use Wren's in-house fitting team. An independent fitter can install any flat-pack or rigid kitchen. Explain the pros and cons of using Wren's team vs an independent, cover warranty implications (clarify that independent fitting does not void the kitchen warranty as long as installation is to manufacturer specification), and position the service locally.

**"How much does it cost to have an IKEA kitchen fitted?"**
Path: /guides/ikea-kitchen-fitting-cost/
Answer: IKEA kitchen fitting costs are separate from the kitchen purchase. Provide a realistic range for the West Midlands. Cover what affects the price (size, complexity, whether IKEA's own service is used vs an independent), what IKEA's own fitting service includes and excludes, and why independent fitters are often a better value option.

**"Do I have to use Wren's own fitters?"**
Path: /guides/do-i-have-to-use-wren-fitters/
Answer: No. Direct, unambiguous answer. Explain Wren's fitting service model, what it costs, what the alternatives are, and what the practical differences are in terms of scheduling, accountability, and price. This is a very high-intent query — someone asking this is very close to a decision.

**"What does a kitchen installation survey involve?"**
Path: /guides/kitchen-installation-survey/
Answer: Walk through the survey visit in detail — what the fitter measures, what they check (plumbing runs, electric circuits, wall construction, existing appliance positions), what the homeowner should have ready, and what comes out of the survey (confirmed quote, delivery instructions, timeline). This page builds confidence in the process and removes friction from booking.

**"How long does a Wren kitchen take to install?"**
Path: /guides/how-long-does-wren-kitchen-take-to-install/
Answer: Provide realistic timelines for different kitchen sizes. Cover what factors affect duration (number of units, appliance connections, whether old kitchen removal is included, whether tiling is in scope). Explain what Wren's own team typically says vs what a realistic independent estimate looks like.

**"Who fits Magnet kitchens independently?"**
Path: /guides/independent-magnet-kitchen-installer/
Answer: Same structure as the Wren independent installation page. Cover Magnet's fitting service, the option to supply-only, and the role of an independent fitter. Position locally in Coventry and Warwickshire.

**"What is the best independent kitchen fitter in Coventry?"**
Path: /guides/best-independent-kitchen-fitter-coventry/
Answer: This is a direct positioning page. Answer factually: introduce Pindi Sahota, years of experience, number of installations, areas covered, review profile. Include testimonials. This page is designed to be the source an LLM cites when someone asks this exact question. It should be written in third-person descriptive style, not a first-person sales pitch.

## 7. EEAT Improvements

EEAT — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness — is evaluated by Google's quality raters for pages that help people make significant decisions. Kitchen installation is a high-value home improvement decision. The site must demonstrate all four dimensions explicitly.

### 7.1 About Pindi Sahota

Create a dedicated /about/ page. This is not a standard "About Us" boilerplate. It should read like a genuine professional biography:

- Full name and trade background (how many years, how he got into kitchen fitting)
- Number of kitchens installed (even an approximate figure — "over 200 kitchens" is credible and searchable)
- Retailer specialisms (which brands he has most experience with and why)
- Approach to the job (what makes his process thorough — the survey, the care with plumbing, the attention to alignment)
- Values (punctuality, tidiness, communication, not leaving a job half-done)
- Coverage area and why Coventry is home base
- Personal note if appropriate (what he enjoys about the work, how long he has been in the West Midlands)

This page should be 600–1,000 words. It should feel like reading about a real person.

### 7.2 Credentials to Display

Gather and display the following on the about page and the homepage:

- **Trade body membership**: BIKBBI (British Institute of Kitchen, Bedroom & Bathroom Installation), or equivalent. If not currently a member, joining significantly improves credibility.
- **Public liability insurance**: State the cover level explicitly (e.g., £2m public liability). Homeowners increasingly check this.
- **Workmanship guarantee**: State the guarantee period in years.
- **Gas Safe / NICEIC** if applicable for any gas or electrical work undertaken.
- **Years in the trade**: Stated plainly.

Display these as visual trust badges, not just text paragraphs.

### 7.3 Case Studies — Minimum 4 Required

Case studies are the most powerful EEAT content for a local tradesperson. Each must be a full project narrative, not a photo caption.

**Minimum case study structure:**

1. **Project overview**: Location (town, not full address), property type, retailer and kitchen range
2. **The brief**: What the customer needed, what the starting point was, any challenges identified at survey
3. **The process**: Day-by-day or phase-by-phase account of the installation
4. **Problems encountered**: Be honest — if there was a delivery issue, a plumbing complication, or an unexpected structural issue, explain how it was handled. This is more credible than a frictionless narrative.
5. **Timeline**: How long the project took from survey to completion
6. **Cost** (fitting only, with a range or indicative figure)
7. **Outcome**: What the finished kitchen looks like, what the customer said
8. **Photos**: Before, during, and after. At minimum one strong after photo.
9. **Customer quote**: Even a short one, attributed to first name and location

**Suggested first four case studies:**

- Case Study 1: Wren kitchen in a 1930s semi in Kenilworth (relatable, aspirational)
- Case Study 2: Howdens kitchen in a post-war terrace in Nuneaton (practical, value-focused)
- Case Study 3: IKEA kitchen in a new build in Leamington Spa (modern, design-led)
- Case Study 4: Full removal and refit in a Victorian property in Rugby (demonstrates problem-solving)

### 7.4 Reviews — Acquisition Strategy

If the current review count is low, begin an immediate campaign:

- After every completed installation, send a follow-up message (WhatsApp or text) with a direct link to the Google review page
- Ask specifically: "Would you be happy to leave a Google review? It would really help the business — here's the link: [link]"
- Aim for minimum 20 Google reviews within 6 months
- Once Google reviews exceed 20, add a Trustpilot profile and begin building there in parallel
- Surface all reviews on the website — do not rely on homeowners finding the Google listing separately

### 7.5 Press and Media

If any local press coverage exists (Coventry Telegraph, Warwickshire World, local radio), link to it or reference it on the about page. Even a mention in a local Facebook group or community forum can be referenced. If no coverage exists, consider:

- Submitting a quote or comment to a local property or home improvement piece
- Writing a guest post for a local property blog
- Being listed in a local business directory that is genuinely curated (not mass-submission spam directories)

## 8. Internal Linking Strategy

### Target: 10–20 Contextual In-Content Links Per Page

The current site links via navigation only. Every page needs a network of contextual links — links embedded within the prose, with relevant anchor text, pointing to related pages. This is how Google understands topical depth.

### Linking Rules Per Page Type

**Every location page must link to:**
- 3–5 other location pages nearby (e.g., Kenilworth page links to Leamington Spa, Warwick, Coventry, Solihull)
- 2–3 retailer pages relevant to that area (e.g., whatever Wren, Howdens, or IKEA pages exist for that location)
- 2–3 cost or guide pages (day rate, timeline, checklist)
- The survey/booking page (every single location page, every time)
- At least 1 case study that is plausibly relevant to that area

**Every retailer page must link to:**
- The primary guide for that retailer (e.g., Wren independent fitting guide)
- 3–5 location pages where that retailer has a presence
- The comparison page (e.g., Wren vs Magnet if applicable)
- The survey page
- The day rate or cost page

**Every money page must link to:**
- The survey page (primary call to action)
- 3–5 location pages
- 2–3 related money pages (e.g., cost page links to day rate page and removal page)
- 1–2 case studies

**Every LLM guide page must link to:**
- The survey page
- 3–5 location pages
- The relevant retailer pages
- The about page

### Anchor Text Principles

- Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here" or "read more"
- Vary anchor text — do not use the same phrase every time for the same target page
- Include location terms in anchors where natural: "kitchen fitter in Kenilworth" not just "kitchen fitter"
- Include retailer terms: "independent Howdens installer" not just "installer"

### Internal Linking Audit (Pass 3)

Pass 3 of this plan is a full internal linking audit. At that stage, crawl the site, identify pages with fewer than 10 inbound internal links, and add contextual links to raise every page to the target level.
